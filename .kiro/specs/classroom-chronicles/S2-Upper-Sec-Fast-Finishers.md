## Scenario Profile
**Level**: Upper Sec  
**Class Profile**: Fast Finishers  
**Auto-derived**: Mathematics / Quadratic factorisation  

**Core tension**: Most students finish tasks quickly but superficially (procedural fluency, weak reasoning). How to check deeper understanding, provide feedback, differentiate challenge? [file:1][file:4][file:5]

## Episode 1 — The moment you see the gap

**Setup**:  
> 25 minutes into Sec 3 Express Math on quadratic factorisation. Modelled \(x^2 + 5x + 6 = (x+2)(x+3)\). Set 4 practice questions. Circulated early.

**Focal student reactions**:  
- Rajan/Alex (fast): all 4 correct fast, doodling.  
- Nur Atiqah/Michelle (fast): correct answers, no justification.  
- Wei Jie (middle): Q1-3 correct slow, Q4 stuck reasoning.  
- Siti (slow): Q1 partial.

**Question**: What do you do first? 20 mins left. [file:3][file:6]

### Choice A: Two-Step MCQ (proof of reasoning)¹²
**Description**: "Q2 \(x^2 + 7x + 12\): (1) Factors? (2) Verify by expanding." All respond.  
**Trade-off**: Exposes procedural vs deep fluency fast, interrupts momentum.¹ [file:6]

### Choice B: Extension justifications (teaching up)³⁷
**Description**: Fast finishers justify "Why these factors?" + "Prove alternate pair impossible." Middle get prompts.  
**Trade-off**: Challenges top without split (Tomlinson up), risks overload.³ [file:4]

### Choice C: Accountable Talk gallery walk⁵⁶
**Description**: Post solutions anonymously; groups critique "Claim correct? Because...". IRF probe.  
**Trade-off**: Builds reasoning publicly, chaos if superficial work dominates.⁵ [file:5][file:6]

**Consequence**:

### A1: Targeted verification reteach (process feedback)⁷¹
**Description**: Model expansion proof; fast do alternates, others verify own.  
**Trade-off**: Hattie process-level for weak verifiers, reteaches fluent.⁷ Serves: Nur/Wei. Underserves: Rajan. [file:5]

### A2: AQS peer on verifications⁸⁵
**Description**: Pairs AQS partner's expansion ("Affirm steps, Question skip, Suggest check").
