## Episode 1 — The moment you see the gap

**Setup**:  
> 20 minutes into Sec 3 Express Math on completing the square. Demonstrated \(x^2 + 8x + 7 = 0\) → \((x+4)^2 = 9\). Set first practice. Circulated. Gap visible.

**Focal student reactions**:  
- Rajan: correct, starting second question.  
- Nur Atiqah: correct first line, stuck halving (sign error).  
- Wei Jie: first line, stares at halving (?).  
- Siti: equation only, nothing else.

**Question**: What do you do first? [file:3]

### Choice A: Two-Step MCQ (AfL check)¹²
**Description**: Project two-step MCQ: 1. \(x^2 + 8x + 7 = 0\) → first step? A) \(x^2 + 4x\) B) \(x^2 + 8x\). 2. Why? A) Halve B) Square full. Hold up/submit before proceeding.  
**Trade-off**: Efficient diagnostic of exact misconception, but consumes practice time.¹ [file:6]

### Choice B: Tiered scaffolds (respectful tasks)³⁴
**Description**: Release scaffolds: full steps (stalled like Siti), prompts (errors like Nur), stretch justification (fast like Rajan). All tasks require step justification.  
**Trade-off**: Immediate ZPD access/dignity, but risks "easy vs hard" perception.³ [file:4]

### Choice C: Accountable Talk pairs (IRF)⁵⁶
**Description**: Pairs explain halving step: "I claim... because...". Committed listener clarifies. Select contrasting pairs for IRF probe.  
**Trade-off**: Surfaces reasoning via talk, but uneven pair quality reinforces errors.⁵ [file:5][file:6]

## Episode 2A — Follows Choice A (Two-Step MCQ)

**Consequence**:  
> 11 students B step 2 (square full coefficient). Wei Jie A/A correct but can't verbalise. Rajan A/A + clear explanation. Siti B/B. Nur A/B. Pattern: halve OK, square wrong. 19 mins left. [file:3][file:6]

### Choice A1: Whole-class reteach (process feedback)⁷¹
**Description**: Address halving-then-square error to all via worked correct/non-example. All re-attempt.  
**Trade-off**: Precise for majority (Hattie process), reteaches secure students.⁷ Serves: middle group. Underserves: Rajan (bored), Siti (passive). [file:5]

### Choice A2: Flex grouping + AQS peer³⁸
**Description**: Rajan-types pair with stalled; AQS on justification ("Affirm strength, Question step, Suggest halve"). Monitor.  
**Trade-off**: Leverages room expertise + structured feedback, Rajan quality variable.³ Serves: Wei Jie. Underserves: mismatched pairs. [file:4][file:6]

## Episode 2B — Follows Choice B (Tiered Scaffolds)

**Consequence**:  
> Wei Jie/Siti take full scaffold gratefully. Wei writes "why minus?". Nur completes prompts but sign error persists. Rajan ignores, independent extension. Quiet room, no class evidence. 18 mins left. [file:3][file:4]

### Choice B1: IRF probe scaffold understanding⁶⁹
**Description**: "Without scaffold, explain halving. Why?" Probe responses whole-class.  
**Trade-off**: Reveals dependency (IRF deepen), exposes fragile grasp publicly.⁶ Serves: Nur self-corrects. Underserves: Siti freezes. [file:5]

### Choice B2: Scaffold removal + self-check¹⁰¹¹
**Description**: Fresh question, no scaffold. "I do → I know → I check" process reflection.  
**Trade-off**: Tests transfer/self-regulation, overwhelms without support.¹⁰ Serves: Rajan. Underserves: stalled group. [file:5][file:6]

## Episode 2C — Follows Choice C (Accountable Talk)

**Consequence**:  
> Rajan clear to partner. Nur pair repeats error mutually. Wei halting to Siti (nods, no write). 3 pairs correct, 5 reinforce misconception. Heard pattern clear, room noisy. 18 mins left. [file:3][file:5]

### Choice C1: AQS on heard error (peer feedback)⁵⁸
**Description**: Board common error; class AQS vs criteria ("Affirm correct, Question square, Suggest halve").  
**Trade-off**: Talk evidence → structured feedback, public error embarrasses sharers.⁵ Serves: middle group. Underserves: quiet. [file:6]

### Choice C2: Solo justify (task evidence)⁷¹²
**Description**: Each writes/justifies halving solo for accountability. Collect.  
**Trade-off**: Prevents borrowed talk, reduces collaborative energy.⁷ Serves: evidence quality. Underserves: participation. [file:5]
